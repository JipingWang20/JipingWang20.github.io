§ 01  /  About

A brief bio.

I'm a Ph.D. candidate in Statistics at Florida State University, specializing in machine learning, deep learning, and tensor data analysis — with a focus on scalable methods for dimension reduction, low-rank regression, classification, and convex optimization.

Before FSU, I studied mathematical statistics at Xiamen University, graduating with honors at both the M.S. and B.S. levels. My Ph.D. research builds interpretable, efficient models for complex, high-dimensional data — tensor regression for EEG seizure detection, hierarchical feature screening for single-cell genomics with 24+ cell types and thousands of genes, and model-free screening for ultra-high-dimensional survival data.

Over the last two summers I interned at Genentech and Ocular Therapeutix, working on clinical-trial design, longitudinal modeling of glaucoma progression, and missing-data methodology — including a design analysis that cut required sample size by 40% for a real trial.

I work in Python (NumPy, scikit-learn, PyTorch), R (tidyverse, caret), and MATLAB, with hands-on experience in exploratory data analysis, model selection, and natural language processing. I'm now looking for full-time roles as a Data Scientist, Applied Scientist, Biostatistician, or Quant Researcher — ideally somewhere interdisciplinary, where rigorous statistics meets impactful data-driven work.

§ 04  /  Research

Scalable methods for structured high-dimensional data.

Thread I

Matrix-Response Regression with Low Tubal Rank and Sparsity

A unified framework integrating low tubal-rank and sparsity constraints for structured high-dimensional data. Designed proximal gradient and linearized ADMM algorithms with convergence guarantees; proved non-asymptotic error bounds and variable selection consistency under restricted strong convexity. Benchmarked against 6 competing methods across 6 simulation settings. Applied to EEG seizure data (19 electrodes, 60 timepoints), identifying P4, C4, T4 as the strongest seizure biomarkers — consistent with established neurological findings.

t-SVD Tubal Rank ADMM EEG In submission
Thread II

Hierarchical Sure Independence Screening for Multiresolution Classification

HierSIS — a scalable feature screening method for high-dimensional multinomial classification that preserves hierarchical response structure, with theoretical guarantees extending Fan & Song (2010). Superior reliability over penalized regression competitors across p ∈ {1500, 2000, 3000, 5000}; competitors exhibit convergence failures in up to 12% of replicates at high dimensions. Applied to the Hao et al. (2021) PBMC single-cell RNA-seq dataset (K = 24 cell types, p up to 5,000 genes) — faster and more stable than mrMLR.

HierSIS Multinomial scRNA-seq PBMC
Thread III

Censored Mean-Variance Screening for Survival Data

A model-free feature screening method for ultra-high-dimensional survival data under censoring, with rigorous theoretical guarantees. Established sure screening and ranking consistency under mild regularity conditions. Simulations demonstrated near-100% true-variable inclusion rates under 20–40% censoring. Applied to breast cancer survival data, achieving the strongest overlap with consensus gene sets and the lowest KS / Cramér–von Mises distance for Cox model compared with competitors.

Screening Survival Cox Model CSDA 2021
